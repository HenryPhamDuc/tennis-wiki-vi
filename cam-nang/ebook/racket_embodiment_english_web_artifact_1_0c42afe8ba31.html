Keep the head up, let the body drive

CORE PRINCIPLE

  • •
    Racket Head UP (keep head above hand after unit turn)
  • •
    Feel the racket as part of your body
  • •
    BODY PUSHES ARM FORWARD → RACKET FOLLOWS
  • •
    No muscle, just natural whip

Unit turn: head up, elbow in line

TWO ADVANCED CONCEPTS

1) SPACE = STRUCTURE

Distance to ball

  • • Ball far → long grip, Big Swing
  • • Ball close → choke up, Compact Swing
  • • On the rise → Compact
  • • On the fall → Big

Grip adjustment

  • • Adjust grip length up/down
  • • Adapt to available space

  • •Feel as one → No need to think
  • •Proactive not reactive

Late acceleration: forearm leads, racket lags

3-SECOND PRE-SHOT CHECKLIST

  1. 1Is racket head above hand?
  2. 2Is right elbow level with shoulders?
  3. 3Is left hand still on throat?
  4. 4Grip relaxed 4/10?
