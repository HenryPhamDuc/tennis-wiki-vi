1 Handle Bevel Alignment

Your notes emphasize the Lateral Plane. For a true Continental grip, the index knuckle must rest on Bevel 2. This sets the palm on a 45° angle, allowing the elbow to extend fully without locking the radioulnar joint.

2 The Kinetic Chain Stack

The total velocity of the racquet head ($v_{head}$) is a sum of angular velocities across the chain. When using the Continental Grip, the "Pronation" phase adds a significant multiplier that is physically impossible in the "Waiter's Tray" position.

Strain Profile Comparison

In the "Waiter's Tray" error, the elbow is flexed ($< 130^\circ$) at impact, causing eccentric loading on the Extensor Carpi Radialis Brevis (ECRB).

Toss Zone & Contact Physics

A toss that is too far forward ($> 50\text{ cm}$) forces the brain to compensate by opening the palm vertically, reverting to the error position.

Corrective Drill Protocol

1. Tyler Twist

Eccentric resistance training for the ECRB using a rolled towel or bar. 3 sets of 15 reps daily.

2. Edge-On Taps

Slow racquet drops. Tap the fence with the racquet edge ONLY to cement the lateral plane feel.

3. Shadow Pronation

Shadow serves focusing on the "Edge-Above-Turn" sequence. Freeze at full $180^\circ$ extension.
