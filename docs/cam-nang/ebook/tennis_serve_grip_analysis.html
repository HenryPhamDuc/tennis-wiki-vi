Continental Setup (Bevel 2)

PROPER MECHANICAL ALIGNMENT

Placing your index knuckle and heel pad on Bevel 2 matches your notebook drawing: "Racquet is above the palm". This aligns the palm to the lateral plane. When you drop the racket behind your head, the wrist has unrestricted freedom to pronate outwards at contact, protecting the joint.

✓ Lateral Wrist Plane: Keeps forearm extensor tendons at rest.
✓ Extended Position: Hand drives upward directly on edge.
✓ Kinetic Delivery: Shoulder rotation creates effortless force.

Biomechanics Summary

Your Hand's Kinetic Path

Following the notes in "Tennis serve grip analysis.docx", switching from a flat, vertical palm (eastern / waiter's tray) to a lateral plane palm (continental) lets the forearm rotate smoothly in a higher arc.

This eliminates local wrist flexing strain, shifts acceleration into shoulder external-to-internal rotation, and protects you against lateral epicondylitis (tennis elbow).
