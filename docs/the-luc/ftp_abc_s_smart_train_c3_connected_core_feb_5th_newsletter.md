Keeping You Fit to PlayTM � ABCs of Tennis Training (C3)

By Carl Petersen BPE, BSc. (PT)

CONNECTING YOUR CORE DURING COVID RESTRICTIONS

During this complicated and trying time you have likely been sitting more
than usual and not as active outdoors. This contributes to poor postural
habits. Ongoing research points to the importance of keeping physically
active during the pandemic so it would be wise to do some preventative
stretching and core training exercises. With facility restrictions and closures
you may be reluctant to hit a gym. So, if you're not getting outside or to
fitness venues now would be time to implement some home core exercise
routines tor a safe and convenient alternative.

Tennis performance requires you to train to improve the body's ability to
stabilize the core and generate power as you move around the court and
swing the racquet. Tennis training requires power, coordination, agility,
acceleration, and deceleration strength in movements passing through
multiple planes of motion and using numerous joints and groups of muscles
at the same time.
Gravity and Rotation

The following exercises help strengthen and stabilize the upper and lower core
muscles in many directions of motion. Following are a series of exercises from our
training resource My Pocket Coach Fitness.

Warm-Up First

Before starting this or any exercise routine do some light dynamic warm up exercises
like leg swings, high knees and crossover runs. Always ensure your core is switched
on like a dimmer switch while doing all exercises.
Seated Horizontal Torso Twists

Training Tips:                                                               position

Reps: 8-10-12, Sets: 1-3
                Supine Bridge & Torso Rotation with Ball Squeeze

Training Tips:
-Start lying with upper back over a large physio/exercise ball
-Place a small ball between knees & squeeze lightly
-Hold a medicine ball or light weight overhead with arms straight
-Switch on your core muscles
-Rotate torso slowly from one side to the other
Reps: 5-10-15, Sets: 1-3
                                   Sumo Squats � Ball at Back

Training Tips:
-Start standing tall in a sumo squat position with a physio ball at your back
& a stretch band in your hands
-Switch on your core muscles
-Squat down slowly keeping knees aligned over toes as you do a diagonal
pull on the stretch band & return to start position
Reps: 10-15-20, Sets: 1-3
                Step Ups with Knee Drive & Diagonal Ball Rotation

Training Tips:
- Stand with one foot on a step holding a medicine or other ball in both
hands
- Switch on your core muscles
- Do a step up and drive one knee up to hip height at the same time doing a
torso rotation raising the ball diagonally
Reps:10-15-20, Sets: 1-3
Resistance Band Walks

These will help improve hip stability for single leg stance to transfer power
from one leg to the other as the player moves into position.
                                         Diagonal Band Walks

Training Tips:
-Start standing in an athletic stance with a stretch band around ankles
-Switch on your core muscles
-Drop or squat slightly & drive one leg back on a diagonal with resistance
from the elastic band
Reps: 5-10-15, Sets: 1-3
Augmenting your strength workouts with these simple exercises using balls
and bands will help you create a stable platform in both the lower and upper
core. This will form a good base of support for quick changes of direction and
rotation during all of your tennis strokes. Good luck and good training. Stay
Fit to PlayTM & Perform both on and off court.
