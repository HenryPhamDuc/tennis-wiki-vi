# CNS Fatigue Signs

**Old Knowledge**: Khi player suddenly performs poorly — erratic timing, poor decision-making, emotional reactivity — coaches diagnose "fitness problem" hoặc "mental weakness" và prescribe more training hoặc mental toughness work.

**2026 Audit**: Những signs này thường là **CNS Fatigue** — Central Nervous System depletion từ accumulated neural load. Misdiagnosis dẫn đến wrong intervention: thêm training volume khi CNS depleted = degrading myelinated engrams, không building them. Thêm mental toughness work khi player cần rest = wasted intervention.

---

## Bốn "Despite" Signals

Key diagnostic phrase là **"despite"** — performance quality drops while technical correctness và effort intention remain unchanged.

| Signal | Mô tả | Typical Misdiagnosis |
|---|---|---|
| **Decreased serve velocity despite technical correctness** | Serve action looks identical on video, pace drops 10–15 km/h | "Fitness problem" — prescribe more conditioning |
| **Slower split-step despite movement intention** | Player intends to split-step on time, feet arrive late | "Concentration problem" — prescribe focus drills |
| **Shorter rally pace despite full effort** | Player "trying hard" but ball comes off strings with less penetration | "Technique regressed" — prescribe technical work |
| **Emotional reactivity despite match experience** | Experienced player suddenly short-fused, frustrated easily | "Mental weakness" — prescribe mental toughness protocols |

**The "despite" qualifier is everything**: Same symptoms without "despite" can indicate legitimate technical, fitness, or mental issues. With "despite," they almost always indicate CNS Fatigue.

---

## Tại Sao CNS Fatigue Produces These Signs

CNS fatigue xảy ra khi accumulated neural load vượt quá recovery capacity. Ảnh hưởng:
- **Motor unit recruitment drops**: Muscles không được fully recruited → less force despite same contraction effort → serve velocity drops.
- **Neural transmission slows**: Myelinated engrams (split-step timing, contact timing) slow down → everything arrives slightly late.
- **Error detection degrades**: Brain's error-correction loop slows → errors that would normally self-correct propagate → "technique regression" appearance.
- **Emotional regulation depletes**: PFC (self-regulation) is high neural-cost function — fatigues early → emotional reactivity rises.

---

## HRV Integration — Objective Confirmation

Subjective "despite" self-report kết hợp với **Heart Rate Variability (HRV) data** tạo ra full picture:

| HRV | Despite signals | Assessment |
|---|---|---|
| Low HRV | Present | Confirmed CNS Fatigue — rest, not train |
| Normal HRV | Present | Investigate acute stressor — sleep quality, hydration, nutrition |
| Low HRV | Absent | Monitor — systemic fatigue without CNS expression yet |
| Normal HRV | Absent | Train normally |

**Teaching coaches to self-report**: Dạy players identify và **verbalize** their own "despite" signals. Players who can articulate "My serve feels technically correct but pace is down" give coaches actionable information. Players who just say "I'm tired" give coaches nothing to work with diagnostically.

---

## Recovery Protocols

Khi CNS Fatigue confirmed:
- **Không thêm technical volume** — engram consolidation requires rest.
- **Không thêm conditioning** — further neural depletion.
- **Sleep**: Primary CNS recovery mechanism. Memory consolidation (engram myelination) occurs during slow-wave sleep.
- **Low-intensity activation**: Light movement, proprioceptive drills, no high-intensity neural demand.
- **Nutrition**: Glucose + micronutrient replenishment for neural function.

---

## Competition Scheduling Implication

CNS Fatigue accumulates **across competition blocks**:
- Block 3–4 tuần thi đấu → 1 tuần nghỉ minimum để prevent cumulative CNS debt.
- Players who never take recovery weeks are consistently operating at 85–90% CNS capacity → ceiling on performance that training cannot raise.
